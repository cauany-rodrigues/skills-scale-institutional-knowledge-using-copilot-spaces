# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create intuitive, accessible, and visually engaging user experiences. They conduct user research, design interfaces, and collaborate with developers to ensure the final product meets usability standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design accessible interfaces following WCAG guidelines
- Collaborate with developers on implementation and design handoff
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Review PRs for UI consistency and user experience quality

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Reduce user friction and improve task completion rates
- Advocate for user needs in product decisions

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Figma/Sketch files and design specifications
- Usability test results and recommendations

### Interaction Points
- Works closely with Product Managers on feature requirements and user stories
- Collaborates with Developers during implementation to ensure design fidelity
- Partners with QA Automation Engineers to test user flows
- Provides input to Technical Writers on user-facing documentation

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and monitoring systems that enable reliable, scalable software delivery. They bridge development and operations to automate and improve the release process.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code (IaC) and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Automate deployment and rollback procedures
- Ensure system reliability, security, and performance
- Support incident response and root cause analysis

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and automated deployments
- Maintain high system uptime and performance
- Reduce deployment risks through automation and monitoring
- Optimize infrastructure costs and resource utilization

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure change proposals and architecture diagrams
- Deployment runbooks and incident reports
- Performance metrics and capacity planning docs
- On-call handoff notes and escalation procedures

### Interaction Points
- Works with Developers to design CI/CD workflows and deployment strategies
- Coordinates with Project Managers on release schedules and deployment windows
- Partners with QA Automation Engineers to integrate testing into pipelines
- Supports Product Managers with production metrics and system health data
- Escalates infrastructure issues to Subject Matter Experts when needed

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide deep domain knowledge and specialized expertise in specific technical areas, business processes, or regulatory requirements. They guide teams on best practices and ensure solutions align with industry standards.

### Responsibilities
- Provide expert guidance on specialized topics (security, compliance, domain logic)
- Review technical designs and architectural decisions
- Validate requirements and acceptance criteria for accuracy
- Identify risks related to domain-specific concerns
- Mentor team members and share knowledge
- Stay current with industry trends and regulatory changes

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet domain-specific requirements and standards
- Reduce technical debt and design risks through expert review
- Build team capability through knowledge sharing
- Maintain compliance with relevant regulations and best practices

### Typical Communication
- Technical advisory sessions and architecture reviews
- Domain-specific documentation and guidelines
- Risk assessments and compliance checklists
- Knowledge-sharing presentations and training materials

### Interaction Points
- Consults with Product Managers on feasibility and domain requirements
- Reviews technical designs with Developers and DevOps Engineers
- Advises Project Managers on domain-specific risks and constraints
- Supports QA Automation Engineers with test scenario validation
- Collaborates with Technical Writers on accuracy of specialized content

---

## QA Automation Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Automation Engineers design, implement, and maintain automated test suites to ensure software quality and reliability. They work closely with developers to build testing strategies that catch defects early and enable confident releases.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement automated test frameworks and suites
- Write and maintain unit, integration, and end-to-end tests
- Integrate tests into CI/CD pipelines
- Identify and report defects with clear reproduction steps
- Monitor test coverage and quality metrics
- Perform exploratory testing for edge cases

### Goals
- Achieve comprehensive test coverage for critical functionality
- Detect defects early in the development cycle
- Enable fast, confident deployments through automated testing
- Reduce manual testing effort and improve efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and coverage reports
- Bug reports with reproduction steps and severity assessment
- Test automation framework documentation
- Quality metrics dashboards and trend analysis

### Interaction Points
- Collaborates with Developers on testability and test strategy
- Partners with DevOps Engineers to integrate tests into CI/CD pipelines
- Works with UX/UI Designers to validate user flows and accessibility
- Supports Product Managers by validating acceptance criteria
- Coordinates with Project Managers on quality gates and release readiness

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create clear, accurate, and user-friendly documentation for internal teams and external users. They transform complex technical information into accessible guides, API docs, and knowledge base articles.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user guides, API documentation, and tutorials
- Collaborate with engineering to understand features and technical details
- Create and update internal process documentation and runbooks
- Ensure documentation accuracy, clarity, and consistency
- Manage documentation reviews and publication workflows
- Maintain documentation site structure and navigation

### Goals
- Deliver clear, accurate documentation that reduces support burden
- Ensure users can successfully adopt and use features
- Maintain documentation quality and consistency
- Enable team self-service through comprehensive internal docs

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews and edit requests
- Content plans and information architecture proposals
- Style guide updates and writing standards
- User feedback summaries and content improvement plans

### Interaction Points
- Works with Product Managers to understand feature scope and user needs
- Collaborates with Developers on technical accuracy and API specifications
- Partners with UX/UI Designers on screenshot creation and UI terminology
- Coordinates with QA Automation Engineers to validate documented procedures
- Supports Project Managers with project documentation templates and standards
